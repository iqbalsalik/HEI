Product Commissioning

Product commissioning includes the process of setting up, testing and fine-tuning newly installed equipment or systems to ensure they function as intended. It typically involves the following steps:

Installation: The equipment is installed according to manufacturer specifications and engineering plans.
Functional Testing: Each component and subsystem is thoroughly tested to ensure they work correctly.
Integration: If the equipment is part of a larger system, it's integrated and tested within that system.
Performance Calibration: The equipment is calibrated to meet performance specifications and regulatory standards.
Safety Checks: Safety features and mechanisms are thoroughly examined to ensure they are functioning correctly.
Documentation: Comprehensive documentation, including test reports and operational manuals, is created to guide users.

Site Commissioning

Site commissioning is a comprehensive process that ensures newly installed or upgraded systems operate effectively and efficiently at their intended location.


Here's a breakdown of the key activities involved in site commissioning:
FAT (Factory Acceptance Test): This initial step involves testing the equipment or system at the factory to verify that it meets design and performance specifications before shipping to the site.
SAT (Site Acceptance Test): After the equipment arrives at the site, it undergoes another round of testing to confirm that it functions correctly within the local environment.
Cable Laying and Termination: Proper installation of electrical and control cables, including termination, to establish the necessary connections.
Installation of Electrical and Control Panels: Mounting and connecting electrical and control panels as per design and safety guidelines.
Cold Commissioning:The system is powered up and tested without the actual production processes, ensuring that all components function as expected.
Hot Commissioning:This phase involves running the system with actual production processes to validate its performance and identify and resolve any issues that may arise.
Performance Test: A series of tests to ensure that the system meets specified performance criteria, including efficiency, accuracy, and reliability.

System Upgradation:If required, any necessary upgrades or modifications to the system are implemented to optimize its performance.
Project Handover Activity:Formal handover of the fully commissioned system to the client, including documentation, training, and necessary support for ongoing operations.

Site commissioning is a critical process to ensure that industrial systems, plants, and equipment are fully operational, safe, and compliant with standards before they become an integral part of the production process. It helps minimize the risk of failures and ensures that the system meets performance expectations.
